<p><b>Arif Saleem Bohru</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-tnm_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tnm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is an Indian <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> better known by his ring name <b>Badshah Khan</b>. Bohru is also the first professional wrestler from <a href="/wiki/Jammu_and_Kashmir_(union_territory)" title="Jammu and Kashmir (union territory)">Jammu and Kashmir</a> region of India.  He currently works in <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a>'s Continental Wrestling Entertainment where he is the current CWE World Heavyweight Champion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  In 2021, Arif is expected to take part in the WWE India tryouts at Mumbai.<sup id="cite_ref-RK_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RK-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dna-india_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dna-india-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zee-news-27_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zee-news-27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Bohru is currently among the top five wrestlers in India.<sup id="cite_ref-zee-news-27_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zee-news-27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badshah_Khan_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_wrestler_from_Jammu_and_Kashmir">First wrestler from Jammu and Kashmir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badshah_Khan_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First wrestler from Jammu and Kashmir">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Badshah Khan became the first professional wrestler from <a href="/wiki/Jammu_and_Kashmir_(union_territory)" title="Jammu and Kashmir (union territory)">Jammu and Kashmir</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is also the first person from Jammu and Kashmir to train under <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a> at CWE academy in Jalandhar.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Continental_Wrestling_Entertainment_.282016-present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Continental_Wrestling_Entertainment_(2016-present)">Continental Wrestling Entertainment (2016-present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badshah_Khan_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Continental Wrestling Entertainment (2016-present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, Arif Bohru entered wrestling through the promotion from <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Khali" title="The Great Khali">The Great Khali</a>'s Continental Wrestling Entertainment. Arif began his training as a professional wrestler. In 2016, Arif Bohru made his wrestling debut under the ring name "Badshah Khan" given to him by The Great Khali.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Arif won the Tag Team Championship twice.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2021)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup id="cite_ref-defyingodds_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-defyingodds-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-emergingwrestler_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-emergingwrestler-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  On 18 March 2018, he won his first CWE World Heavyweight Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-defyingodds_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-defyingodds-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-emergingwrestler_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-emergingwrestler-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2021, Khan is set to debut in the World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE).<sup id="cite_ref-zee-news-27_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zee-news-27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badshah_Khan_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arif Bohru hails from Neel, <a href="/wiki/Ramban_district" title="Ramban district">Ramban district</a>. His father, Mohammed Saleem Bohru,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Central_Reserve_Police_Force" title="Central Reserve Police Force">CRPF</a> sub-inspector.<sup id="cite_ref-zee-news-27_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zee-news-27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
