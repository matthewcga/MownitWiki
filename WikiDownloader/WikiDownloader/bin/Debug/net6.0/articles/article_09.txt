<p><b>Września railway station</b> (German: <i>Wreschen</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">railway station</a> serving the town of <a href="/wiki/Wrze%C5%9Bnia" title="Września">Września</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Poland_Voivodeship" title="Greater Poland Voivodeship">Greater Poland Voivodeship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. The station is located on the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw%E2%80%93Kunowice_railway" title="Warsaw–Kunowice railway">Warsaw–Kunowice railway</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ole%C5%9Bnica%E2%80%93Chojnice_railway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oleśnica–Chojnice railway (page does not exist)">Oleśnica–Chojnice railway</a>. The train services are operated by <a href="/wiki/Polish_State_Railways" title="Polish State Railways">PKP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Koleje_Wielkopolskie" title="Koleje Wielkopolskie">Koleje Wielkopolskie</a>.
</p><p>The station is categorized as a grade D station. In the 1960s the station's red brick structure was plastered and repainted in beige, and the inside was completely renovated. The renovation of the façade was undertaken in December 2008. In the past, the railway station used to have a <a href="/wiki/Water_towers_in_Wrze%C5%9Bnia#Tower_on_the_railroad_station" title="Water towers in Września">water tower</a>.
</p><p>Railway line number 3 between Warsaw, Poznań and <a href="/wiki/Kunowice" title="Kunowice">Kunowice</a> passes about 1.5&#160;km north of Września station. The station is located on a branch of this line which serves the station. The station's switchboards control connections of the number 807 and 808 lines.
</p><p>Train services between Gniezno, Września and Jarocin were suspended in December 2012, but they were reinstated in June 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
