<p><i><b>Coccothrinax torrida</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Arecaceae" title="Arecaceae">palm</a> <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to southeastern <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Morici_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morici-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It is known from a single isolated hill on the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Province" title="Guantánamo Province">Guantánamo Province</a>.  The climate of the area is described as <a href="/wiki/Cuban_cactus_scrub" title="Cuban cactus scrub">semi-desert</a> with 9 or 10 dry months.<sup id="cite_ref-Morici_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morici-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Like other members of the genus, <i>C. torrida</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Fan_palm" title="Fan palm">fan palm</a>.  Trees are single-stemmed, between 2 and 8 m tall with stems 2.5 to 3.9 (occasionally up to 4.5) cm in diameter.  The fruit is creamy white, 4.8–6.3&#160;mm in diameter.<sup id="cite_ref-Morici_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morici-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The species was first described in 2006 from material collected in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-Morici_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morici-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It is related to <i><a href="/wiki/Coccothrinax_pauciramosa" title="Coccothrinax pauciramosa">C. pauciramosa</a></i>, and is characterized by the following features: narrow semiorbicular flat leaves that are covered on the adaxial surface by white wax; a small irregular palman; long and erect inflorescences; and small, white, smooth fruits. <i>C. torrida</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Coccothrinax_montana" title="Coccothrinax montana">C. montana</a></i> are the only described species with white fruits in the genus <i>Coccothrinax</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Morici_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morici-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Since it is known from a single population of about 5000 individuals, the species is considered <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">Vulnerable</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Morici_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morici-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
