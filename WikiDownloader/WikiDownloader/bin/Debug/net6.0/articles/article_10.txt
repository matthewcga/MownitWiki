<p><b>Ambarish Rai</b> was an Indian Right to Education activist. He was the founder and national convener of the Right To Education (RTE) Forum, the largest civil society platform on education in India.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambarish_Rai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rai was a law graduate from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Lucknow" title="University of Lucknow">University of Lucknow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During his university time, he was elected as general secretary of <a href="/wiki/All_India_Students_Federation" class="mw-redirect" title="All India Students Federation">All India Students’ Federation</a>, a student organization.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambarish_Rai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a student, Rai protested against the dual system of education in India<i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was involved in activism with AISF and <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_India" title="Communist Party of India">Communist Party of India (CPI)</a> in UP and later worked as a member of <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_India_(Marxist%E2%80%93Leninist)_Liberation" title="Communist Party of India (Marxist–Leninist) Liberation">CPI(ML)</a>'s UP State Committee. He worked with the <a href="/wiki/Indian_People%27s_Front" title="Indian People&#39;s Front">Indian People's Front</a> and <a href="/wiki/All_India_Central_Council_of_Trade_Unions" title="All India Central Council of Trade Unions">All India Central Council of Trade Unions</a> in the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was appointed the national organizer for National Alliance for the Fundamental Right to Education (NAFRE) and later joined the People's Campaign for Common School System (PCCSS) in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As part of the organizations, he played an instrumental role in increasing the demand for education to be made a fundamental right, leading to the 86th constitutional amendment aimed to "provide free and compulsory education of all children in the age group of six to fourteen years" as a Fundamental Right and the legislation of the <a href="/wiki/Right_of_Children_to_Free_and_Compulsory_Education_Act,_2009" title="Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009">Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Rai mobilized Tribals in Gujarat and Maharashtra for a movement leading to the enactment of <a href="/wiki/The_Scheduled_Tribes_and_Other_Traditional_Forest_Dwellers_(Recognition_of_Forest_Rights)_Act,_2006" title="The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006">Forest Rights Act, 2006</a> and was the president of Lok Sangharsh Morcha.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, he formed the RTE Forum — a coalition of 10,000 educators, nonprofits, teachers’ unions, networks and community members — a platform aiming to achieve full realization of the Right to Education Act.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, he was chosen as a <a href="/wiki/Malala_Fund#Education_Champion_Network" title="Malala Fund">Malala Fund Education Champion</a> and worked towards advancing girls' education in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Contrary to increasing privatization efforts, Rai advocated an increase in allocation for education in the budget. He was a supporter of the no detention policy and felt that students cannot be blamed for systemic failures.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambarish_Rai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rai died on 23 April 2021 in Delhi with Covid-like symptoms and was a victim of Oxygen shortage.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
